June 3:
From looking at my code and what I see with the stocks I'm looking at overall, the past year was very bullish for these stocks,
and the prices rose, which makes B&H simply really hard to beat. So, I decided to change it so that the benchmark we're looking at
is now a stock; right now I'm looking at either making it SPY or QQQ. From the data now, I get positive returns on most stocks;
however, there are. few stocks, like CRM and WIX, that our strategy does quite poorly on. I tried implementing adx (average
directional index), which helped bring our return to +30.49% and winrate to +62.24%! However, the alpha (now calculated in
comparison to our benchmark stock) was still not great. I've also realized something: In an ideal simulation, on each day the 
simulation takes a look and allocates its money to a stock. In this case, my average returns don't really mean anything 
because it's not like all of these are going on simultaneously. In the real world, sometimes the model will recognize that 
the stock just isn't good and won't buy it, and spend money on other things in its portfolio instead. Trying to get a stock 
that was going down to give positive returns every time just isn't realistic. So, tomorrow I'll try to make a change to my model to 
make it take in every single one of these stocks as potential options, and decide which position is best to hold.
